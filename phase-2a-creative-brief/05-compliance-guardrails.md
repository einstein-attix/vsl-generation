## Regulatory Framework

### Three Governing Bodies

| Regulator | What They Care About | Risk Level | Consequence |
|-----------|---------------------|------------|-------------|
| **SEC** (Securities and Exchange Commission) | Whether we're giving "investment advice" without registration; whether performance claims are misleading | HIGH | Cease-and-desist, fines, criminal referral for fraud |
| **FTC** (Federal Trade Commission) | Whether advertising claims are truthful; whether endorsements are disclosed; whether subscription billing is transparent | MEDIUM | Fines ($50K+ per violation), forced refunds, injunctions |
| **State regulators** | Varies by state — investment advisor registration, consumer protection | LOW–MEDIUM | State-specific enforcement actions |

### The Critical Legal Distinction: Publisher vs. Investment Advisor

**We are a PUBLISHER. We are NOT an investment advisor.**

This is the single most important legal boundary. It determines everything we can and cannot say.

| Publisher (What We Are) | Investment Advisor (What We Are NOT) |
|------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Provides general information about securities | Provides personalized investment advice |
| Makes observations about market trends | Recommends specific actions for specific people |
| Shares data and analysis for educational purposes | Manages money or directs trades |
| Protected by First Amendment | Requires SEC registration (RIA) |
| Example: "The AI holds NVDA with a 91/100 score" | Example: "You should buy NVDA" |

**SEC precedent:** *Lowe v. SEC* (1985) — Supreme Court ruled that publishers of investment newsletters are protected by the First Amendment and are NOT investment advisors as long as they do not provide personalized advice. This is our legal foundation.

**SEC warning:** *SEC v. Stansberry & Associates* (2007) — Stansberry was fined $1.5M for fraud because they made materially false claims about specific securities (claimed insider knowledge they didn't have). Our protection: every claim we make is backed by verifiable, timestamped data. We never claim insider knowledge or guaranteed outcomes.

## The 10 Compliance Commandments

### 1. NEVER Make Forward-Looking Performance Claims

**Forbidden:**
- "The AI will make you money"
- "You'll see X% returns"
- "This portfolio is going to outperform the market"
- "Your investment will grow"
- "Expect returns of..."

**Allowed:**
- "The AI's portfolio returned X% over the past 11 months"
- "Here's what the AI did last quarter — including the losing trades"
- "488 documented trades with verified timestamps"
- "Past performance, including the results shown here, does not guarantee future results"

**The rule:** Always past tense. Always verifiable. Always accompanied by the appropriate disclaimer.

### 2. NEVER Give Personalized Investment Advice

**Forbidden:**
- "You should buy NVDA"
- "I recommend selling your index funds"
- "Based on your situation, you need this"
- "Invest in the AI's portfolio"
- "Follow these trades"

**Allowed:**
- "The AI currently holds NVDA with a score of 91/100"
- "Signal subscribers can see every trade the AI makes"
- "The portfolio data is available for subscribers to review"
- "Some investors use this data alongside their own research"

**The rule:** We show data. We do not direct action. The subscriber decides what to do with the information.

### 3. ALWAYS Include Required Disclaimers

**Every piece of marketing (VSL, landing page, email, ad) must include:**

**Primary disclaimer (must be visible/audible):**
> "Past performance is not indicative of future results. The Signal provides portfolio data and market analysis for informational and educational purposes only. This is not investment advice. All investing involves risk, including the potential loss of principal. You should consult a licensed financial advisor before making any investment decisions."

**Performance disclaimer (when showing returns/P&L):**
> "Performance data shown reflects the actual results of AI-managed model portfolios. These results include all trades, both winning and losing. Individual results may vary. Past performance does not guarantee future results."

**Subscription disclaimer (at point of purchase):**
> "The Signal is an annual subscription that auto-renews at the then-current rate. You may cancel at any time. Refund policy: full refund within 60 days of initial purchase."

**Placement requirements:**
- **VSL:** Disclaimer text on screen during performance data display AND voiceover at least once. Must be legible (min 12pt on screen) and not hidden in fine print.
- **Landing page:** Above the fold AND at bottom of page before CTA.
- **Email:** Footer of every email.
- **Ads:** In ad copy or first screen of landing page.

### 4. NEVER Use Fake Scarcity or Manufactured Urgency

**Forbidden:**
- "Only 500 spots available" (for a digital product — obviously false)
- "This offer expires in 24 hours" (unless it genuinely does and never returns)
- "Prices going up at midnight" (unless they genuinely are)
- "Limited time only" (vague, potentially misleading)
- Countdown timers that reset on page reload

**Allowed:**
- "Introductory pricing for new subscribers" (if genuinely introductory)
- "The AI traded 3 times this week — Signal subscribers saw every one" (authentic FOMO)
- "Join [number] subscribers who are already watching the AI trade" (social proof, if accurate)

**The rule:** All urgency must be grounded in reality. The AI trading in real-time creates authentic urgency — we don't need to manufacture it.

### 5. NEVER Fabricate or Exaggerate Data

**Forbidden:**
- Rounding "487 trades" up to "almost 500 trades"
- Showing a 11-month chart that visually exaggerates returns
- Cherry-picking the best-performing portfolio without showing all 5
- Cropping charts to hide underperformance periods
- Using hypothetical or backtested data without clearly labeling it as such

**Allowed:**
- Exact numbers: "488 rebalancing events" (not "nearly 500")
- Complete data: all 5 portfolios, all time periods, all losses
- Charts with accurate axis scaling
- Backtested data clearly labeled: "This is a simulation, not live trading data"

**The rule:** If it's not exactly accurate, don't say it. Our entire positioning is built on transparency — a single exaggeration destroys everything.

### 6. NEVER Imply Guaranteed Income or Risk-Free Returns

**Forbidden:**
- "Risk-free investing"
- "Guaranteed returns"
- "You can't lose"
- "Safe investment"
- "The AI eliminates risk"
- "Replace your income with..."

**Allowed:**
- "All investing involves risk, including the loss of principal"
- "The AI has both winning and losing trades — we show both"
- "Diversification across 5 portfolios and 8 strategies is designed to manage, but not eliminate, risk"

**The rule:** Risk acknowledgment must be genuine and prominent, not buried in fine print.

### 7. ALWAYS Distinguish Between AI Portfolio Data and Personal Advice

**Forbidden:**
- Implying subscribers should mirror the AI's trades ("copy every move")
- Suggesting the AI's positions are suitable for any specific individual
- Framing the newsletter as a "managed account" at the $49 tier

**Allowed:**
- "See what the AI is doing"
- "The portfolio data is updated daily"
- "Subscribers use this data as one input in their own investment research"

**The rule:** The newsletter publishes data. What the subscriber does with that data is their decision. This distinction must be explicit and repeated.

### 8. ALWAYS Honor the Guarantee — No Deceptive Billing

**Required:**
- 60-day money-back guarantee must be unconditional (no "valid reasons" gate)
- Cancellation process must be simple (email or one-click, not phone-only)
- Auto-renewal must be clearly disclosed before purchase
- Renewal price must be stated if different from introductory price
- No "negative option" billing (charging for something they didn't explicitly agree to)

**FTC focus areas (2025–2026):**
- The FTC's "Click to Cancel" rule (effective 2025) requires cancellation to be as easy as signup
- Subscription billing transparency — renewal terms must be conspicuous at checkout
- Dark patterns in checkout flows are under active enforcement

### 9. NEVER Use Misleading Testimonials or Social Proof

**Forbidden:**
- Fabricated testimonials
- Testimonials that represent atypical results without disclaimer
- Paid endorsements without disclosure
- Fake subscriber counts or engagement metrics
- AI-generated fake reviews

**Allowed:**
- Genuine subscriber testimonials with: (a) real name or verified identity, (b) disclaimer that results are not typical if they represent exceptional outcomes, (c) FTC disclosure if compensated
- Accurate subscriber counts ("Join 10,000+ subscribers" — only if true)
- Media mentions (with permission)

**Since we don't have subscriber testimonials yet:** The track record IS the testimonial. We don't need human endorsements when we have 488 verifiable trades. Don't fabricate social proof — build it organically.

### 10. NEVER Position the Newsletter as a Registered Investment Service

**Forbidden:**
- "We manage your portfolio"
- "Our investment recommendations"
- "Advisory service"
- "Financial planning"
- "Fiduciary"
- Any language implying RIA registration

**Allowed:**
- "Newsletter" / "publication" / "research service" / "market intelligence"
- "Educational content"
- "Portfolio data and analysis"
- "Informational purposes"

**The managed accounts tier ($50K+) IS an RIA-regulated service** — but that's a separate entity with separate compliance. The $49 newsletter must be clearly distinguished from the managed accounts business.

## VSL-Specific Compliance Checklist

Before the VSL script goes to production, every claim must pass this checklist:

### Performance Claims
- [ ] Every return number is sourced from actual portfolio data
- [ ] Time period is specified (not just "the AI returned X%")
- [ ] Disclaimer "past performance does not guarantee future results" appears on-screen and in voiceover
- [ ] Losing trades are shown alongside winning trades
- [ ] Benchmark comparison uses accurate, matching time periods
- [ ] No implied forward-looking performance

### Language Audit
- [ ] Zero instances of "you should buy/sell"
- [ ] Zero instances of "guaranteed" or "risk-free"
- [ ] Zero instances of "will" regarding future performance (use "did" / "has")
- [ ] Zero instances of fake urgency or scarcity
- [ ] All subscriber counts and engagement metrics are accurate
- [ ] No competitor claims that can't be independently verified

### Offer Compliance
- [ ] Price is clearly stated
- [ ] Auto-renewal terms are disclosed before purchase
- [ ] Renewal price stated if different from introductory
- [ ] Guarantee terms are specific and honored
- [ ] Cancellation process is disclosed
- [ ] No hidden fees or surprise charges

### Disclaimer Placement
- [ ] On-screen disclaimer during all performance data displays
- [ ] Voiceover disclaimer at least once during proof section
- [ ] Full disclaimer text on landing page (above fold + before CTA)
- [ ] Disclaimer in checkout flow
- [ ] "Not investment advice" language in email footer

## Attorney Review Checkpoints

The MASTERPLAN specifies attorney review built into the process. Here are the required review gates:

| Checkpoint | When | What's Reviewed |
|------------|------|-----------------|
| **Pre-script** | Before writing begins | This compliance document + offer terms |
| **Script draft** | After first draft complete | Every claim, every disclaimer, every promise |
| **Final script** | After optimization pass | Full read-through for any new compliance issues introduced during editing |
| **Landing page** | Before launch | Offer terms, billing disclosure, guarantee language, disclaimers |
| **Ad creative** | Before ad spend begins | Claims in ads match approved VSL claims |
| **Quarterly review** | Every 90 days | Updated performance data claims, regulatory changes |

## Red Lines — Immediate Stop if Violated

If any of these appear in any marketing material, production stops until resolved:

1. **Forward-looking return promises** — "You'll make X%"
2. **Personalized advice language** — "You should buy/sell"
3. **Fake data** — Any fabricated or exaggerated performance number
4. **Missing disclaimers** — Performance shown without required disclaimer
5. **Undisclosed billing** — Auto-renewal not disclosed before purchase
6. **Fake scarcity** — "Only X spots available" for a digital product
7. **Fabricated testimonials** — Reviews or endorsements that aren't genuine
8. **RIA language** — "Advisory" / "fiduciary" / "we manage your money" (at the $49 tier)

## The Compliance Advantage

Here's the reframe: compliance isn't limiting our VSL — it's *strengthening* it.

Every compliance guardrail aligns with our positioning:
- "Show past performance, not future promises" → Our proof-first strategy
- "Include losses" → Our radical transparency differentiator
- "Don't give personalized advice" → We publish data, not opinions
- "No fake scarcity" → Our authentic urgency (AI trades in real-time)
- "Clear disclaimers" → Trust-building, not trust-eroding

A compliant Proof Lead VSL is actually *more* persuasive than a non-compliant hype VSL — because the compliance structures force us to show evidence instead of making promises. And evidence is what our target avatar (high-income, skeptical professional) responds to.

**The competitors who cut compliance corners are the ones who build fragile businesses.** Stansberry's $1.5M SEC fine. Agora's repeated legal issues. FTC crackdowns on subscription billing. We build a VSL that's so clean, so transparent, so evidence-based that compliance review is a formality, not a fight.
