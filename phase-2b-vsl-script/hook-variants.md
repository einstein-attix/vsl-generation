## How to Use This Document

Each hook is a self-contained 2-minute opening (approximately 280–320 words at measured pace). They all funnel into the same Problem section at minute 2:00. Test each hook against cold traffic. The winning hook becomes the permanent cold open; runners-up become retargeting variants and ad hooks.

## Hook #5: The Curiosity Hook
### Angle: "What happens when you remove humans from trading?"
### Target emotion: Intellectual curiosity → "I have to see how this ends"

**[VISUAL: Two trading dashboards side by side. Label on left: "Human Analyst." Label on right: "AI Portfolio Manager." Both show the same starting date.]**

**NARRATOR** (thoughtful, documentary-style):

Here's an experiment that shouldn't surprise anyone — but it will.

Take a human stock analyst. Give them every tool available — Bloomberg terminal, research team, 20 years of experience. Let them pick stocks for 11 months.

Now take an AI. Give it the same data — fundamentals, momentum, institutional flow, risk metrics — but strip out everything human. No ego. No attachment to a losing position. No "I like this company." No anchoring to the price they paid.

Let them both run for 11 months.

**[VISUAL: Both dashboards animate forward through time. The AI dashboard shows more frequent adjustments — weekly rebalances. The human dashboard shows fewer changes.]**

What happens is exactly what you'd expect — and nothing like what you've been told.

The AI doesn't pick "the next Amazon." It doesn't make bold predictions. It doesn't have a "secret system."

It scores every security from 0 to 100. It rebalances every week. It exits positions when the data changes — not when it feels like it. It re-enters 37% of the positions it previously sold, because the math changed again.

And it documents everything. Every entry. Every exit. Every loss.

**[VISUAL: Counter animating up — 488 documented trades.]**

Four hundred eighty-eight trades. Five portfolios. Zero emotion.

The results? I'll show you all of them — including every trade that lost money. Because that's the part nobody else will show you, and it's the part that makes everything else believable.

## Hook Testing Framework

### Recommended Test Protocol

| Variable | Setting |
|----------|---------|
| **Platform** | YouTube pre-roll (15s skip test) + Meta (thumb-stop test) |
| **Budget** | $500/hook × 5 hooks = $2,500 total test budget |
| **Metric** | 15-second view rate (YouTube), thumb-stop rate (Meta), VSL page CTR |
| **Duration** | 7 days per hook, 1,000+ impressions each |
| **Winner criteria** | Highest VSL page CTR × 25% watch rate |

### Predicted Performance Ranking

1. **Hook #1 (Proof-First)** — Most aligned with our positioning. The silent dashboard opening is a genuine pattern interrupt. Predicted strongest with cold skeptical traffic.
2. **Hook #4 (Contrarian)** — Strong for audiences burned by competitors. Best for retargeting people who've visited Motley Fool or Stansberry.
3. **Hook #5 (Curiosity)** — Best intellectual engagement. Will attract highest-quality subscribers (lowest churn). Documentary tone builds trust fast.
4. **Hook #2 (Fear)** — Proven format in financial publishing. Will have highest raw view rates but potentially lower quality subscribers.
5. **Hook #3 (Greed)** — Most conventional. Will convert but attracts greed-motivated subscribers who churn faster.

### Combination Strategy

The hooks are designed to be modular. In production:
- **Cold traffic:** Hook #1 (Proof-First) as primary, Hook #4 (Contrarian) as variant
- **Retargeting (visited but didn't convert):** Hook #3 (Greed) — show specific recent trades
- **Lookalike audiences (competitor subscribers):** Hook #4 (Contrarian) — validates their frustration
- **High-income professional targeting:** Hook #5 (Curiosity) — matches analytical mindset
- **55+ demographic:** Hook #2 (Fear) with softer tone — protection framing
