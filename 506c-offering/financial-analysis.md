# TA FINTECH, INC. — FINANCIAL ANALYSIS

## Period: FY 2025 vs FY 2024
### Source: Unaudited Financial Statements, December 31, 2025
### Prepared: March 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

TA Fintech, Inc. (TradeAlgo) is a pre-profit, growth-stage fintech company generating $12.9M in annual revenue from subscription-based software services. The company shows meaningful operational improvement in FY2025: revenue grew 34.6%, gross margins expanded to 71.8%, operating losses narrowed by 19.6%, and total operating expenses declined 11.5% despite the revenue growth.

However, the balance sheet has tightened significantly. Cash fell from $21.8M to $1.8M, equity eroded from $21.0M to $0.9M, and the company raised only $15.4M in new equity versus $63.1M the prior year. With a monthly operating cash burn of ~$2.2M, cash runway on hand is approximately 1 month — though $12.8M in liquid equity securities extends the effective runway to ~6.5 months. The company will require additional capital in 2026 to sustain operations.

Deferred revenue more than doubled to $15.1M, signaling strong forward bookings and subscription momentum that has not yet been recognized as revenue.

### Revenue

- **34.6% YoY growth** — healthy for a subscription SaaS business at this stage.
- Revenue recognized is only a portion of cash collected — deferred revenue grew $8.2M, meaning the company collected ~$21.1M in subscription cash during 2025 ($12.9M recognized + $8.2M deferred increase).
- **Implied total bookings for 2025: ~$21.1M** — this is the more representative demand signal.

### Cost Structure

- **Gross margin expanded from 65.9% to 71.8%** — COGS grew only 11.4% while revenue grew 34.6%, showing operating leverage in the delivery model.
- **G&A reduced 38.8%** ($21.3M → $13.0M) — the single largest expense improvement. This suggests headcount or overhead reductions.
- **Sales & Marketing reduced 62.6%** ($3.3M → $1.2M) — aggressive cut. This may reflect a shift to sales-led conversion (reducing paid acquisition) or a conscious decision to reduce spend during a capital-constrained period.
- **R&D increased 16.0%** ($27.2M → $31.6M) — the company is investing more in product development. R&D is 244% of recognized revenue, which is extremely high but consistent with a company building AI/ML platforms with long development cycles.
- **Total operating expenses reduced 11.5%** despite revenue growth — a meaningful efficiency improvement.

### Profitability

- **Operating loss narrowed by $8.9M** (19.6% improvement).
- **Net loss narrowed by $13.7M** (27.9% improvement). Note: FY2024 included a $4.1M tax provision that did not recur in FY2025.
- **Adjusted net loss improvement** (excluding the 2024 tax provision): $9.6M improvement, or 21.3%.
- The company remains deeply unprofitable — net loss is 274% of revenue — but the trajectory is improving.

### Other Income

- **Other income tripled** from $321K to $1.0M — likely driven by gains or dividends on the equity securities portfolio and/or interest income.

## 7. Key Observations

### Positive Signals

1. **Revenue growing 34.6%** with improving gross margins (65.9% → 71.8%). The SaaS model shows operating leverage.
2. **Deferred revenue doubled** to $15.1M — the strongest leading indicator. This represents cash already collected and contractually committed. Implied bookings growth of ~49%.
3. **Operating loss narrowed 19.6%** despite continued heavy R&D investment. Total OpEx reduced 11.5%.
4. **Zero financial debt.** All liabilities are operational (deferred revenue, payables). No interest expense.
5. **G&A cut 38.8%** — evidence of cost discipline and overhead rationalization.
6. **2025 equity round at 45% premium** to 2024 pricing — suggests valuation growth between rounds.
7. **Cash burn improved 34.1%** — from $3.4M/month to $2.2M/month in operating cash outflows.

### Risk Factors

1. **Cash position critically low.** $1.8M cash with $2.2M/month burn = ~0.8 months runway. Even with $12.8M in equity securities, adjusted runway is ~6.5 months. The company must raise capital or achieve dramatic cost reductions in H1 2026.
2. **Accumulated deficit of $108M.** The company has consumed nearly all capital ever raised. Total equity is $0.9M — close to the threshold where equity could turn negative.
3. **R&D at 244% of revenue** — while common in early-stage AI/ML companies, this ratio must decline significantly for the company to approach breakeven. At current revenue, R&D alone would need to drop by ~70% to reach operating breakeven (holding all else equal).
4. **Fundraising pace slowed dramatically.** $15.4M raised in 2025 vs $63.1M in 2024 (75.7% decline). If the company cannot raise at a similar or accelerated pace in 2026, operations may be constrained.
5. **Sales & Marketing cut 62.6%** — while this reduces burn, it may also constrain customer acquisition and revenue growth. The company appears to be conserving capital at the expense of growth spend.
6. **Accounts receivable growth (377%) outpaces revenue growth (35%)** — warrants monitoring for collection risk or extended payment terms.
7. **Equity securities concentration risk.** $12.8M in Vanguard securities provides liquidity buffer but introduces market risk. A significant market downturn would impair this safety net.
8. **Unaudited financials.** FY2025 statements are unaudited. Material adjustments could emerge during audit.

### Path to Sustainability

For context, the company would need to close the $36.5M operating loss gap. Paths include:

- **Revenue scale:** At 71.8% gross margin, the company needs ~$63.8M in revenue to cover current OpEx ($45.8M / 0.718). This implies ~5x current revenue.
- **Cost reduction:** Alternatively, reducing OpEx to ~$9.3M (current gross profit) while maintaining revenue. This would require cutting R&D by ~70%.
- **Hybrid:** Most likely path — revenue doubles while OpEx grows at a slower rate, narrowing losses until a future round or profitability.
