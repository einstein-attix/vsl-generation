## 1. THE BIG IDEA

**Current (implied):** "You already use TradeAlgo — now you can own it." — 5/10. It's logical but not electric.

**What it SHOULD be:**

> "You're sitting on the inside of the next Bloomberg — and the rest of Wall Street doesn't know it yet."

> "The 50,000 people who use TradeAlgo every day are the only investors in the world who can evaluate this company from the inside out. Wall Street hasn't priced this. The public markets haven't seen it. You have. This is the rarest thing in investing: an information advantage that's perfectly legal."

This turns product usage into an **informational edge**. The Agora "Insider's Edge" frame.

## 2. THE DESTABILIZING TRUTH — Missing Entirely

**Option A (Strongest):** "Bloomberg charges $32K/year for what TradeAlgo delivers — and Bloomberg has ZERO auditable AI trading history. The $32K product is actually LESS transparent than yours."

**Option B:** "Fifteen million dollars is already IN the bank as deferred revenue. Even if TradeAlgo never sold another subscription, they'd recognize $15M over the next 12-36 months."

**Option C:** "Every financial newsletter gives you opinions. TradeAlgo gives you receipts — 488 of them, including every loss. The entire $200B financial media industry is built on 'trust us.' TradeAlgo is built on 'verify us.'"

## 5. INSIDER vs. MARK — The #1 Existential Threat

The viewer sees: never profitable, $107.9M deficit, SEC investigation, 88.7X multiple, one person controls 56%, $1.8M cash with $2.2M/month burn.

**An unsophisticated viewer thinks: "They need my money to survive."**

**Fixes — The "Velvet Rope" Technique:**

**A. Emphasize EXCLUSION:** "This isn't on Robinhood. It's not on Schwab. Only verified accredited investors can participate. Most people who WANT to invest literally cannot."

**B. Reframe capital raise as OFFENSIVE:** "This isn't about keeping the lights on. 35% goes to AI engineering. 15% to infrastructure. This is growth capital for a company that already has $12.9M in revenue."

**C. "You know something they don't":** "Most investors evaluate from the outside. You've been on the inside. You've USED TradeGPT. You already did your due diligence — you just didn't know it yet."

**D. Name the skepticism:** "You might be thinking: if this company is so great, why do they need my money? It's a fair question. Here's the answer..."

## 7. FIVE REWRITES

### 1. The Opening
Replace "You already know what TradeAlgo does" with: "Fifty thousand people use a platform every day that Bloomberg can't replicate at thirty-two thousand a year. You're one of them. For the first time, the company is opening its cap table to the people who know it best."

### 2. The Deferred Revenue Moment
Replace flat data with: "Fifteen million dollars is already collected. Already in the bank. For services subscribers already signed up for. Even if we never sold another subscription, that fifteen million gets recognized over the next three years."

### 3. The Competitor Kill Shot
Replace the 3-column layout with: "Bloomberg: $32K/year, zero AI trading transparency. Motley Fool: opinions with no receipts. Betterment: passive with no intelligence. TradeAlgo: $2,995/year, 488 auditable AI events, institutional data. The question isn't whether TradeAlgo competes. The question is whether the rest of the industry catches up before you invest."

### 4. The Risk Reframe
Add before risk section: "Let me do something most investment presentations don't do — I'm going to tell you every reason NOT to invest. Because if you can hear all of this and still want in, you'll invest with conviction, not hope."

### 5. The Identity CTA
Replace generic CTA with: "You've been a TradeAlgo customer. A subscriber. A user. Today you can become something else: an owner. The question is simple — do you want to keep paying for the data... or do you want to profit from everyone who does?"
