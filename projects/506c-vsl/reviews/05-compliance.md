# ⚖️ 506(c) SECURITIES COMPLIANCE REVIEW

## TA Fintech Inc. — Private Investment VSL Script

---

## OVERALL COMPLIANCE RATING: 6/10

Better than most promotional investment videos. Risk disclosure is unusually robust. SEC investigation IS disclosed. However, **critical deficiencies** expose the company to enforcement risk, especially given the **active investigation**. When you're under the microscope, every word matters.

---

## I. MUST-FIX ITEMS (Do Not Publish Without These)

### MUST-FIX #1: SEC Investigation Disclosure Is Buried

**Problem:** Doesn't appear until 71% through (Slide 39, minute 12:40) — after 12+ minutes of promotional content. Under Rule 10b-5, the *placement* and *prominence* of material disclosures matters.

**Fix:**
1. Add SEC investigation disclosure to Scene 1 (Slide 6): "TA Fintech is currently under investigation by the SEC. This is a material risk. I'll provide more detail later, but you should be aware from the outset."
2. Add persistent on-screen text overlay from Slide 7 forward
3. Keep Slide 39 detailed disclosure, but it must NOT be the first mention

### MUST-FIX #2: The "40x Story" Is Potentially Misleading

**Problem:** "Roughly a forty X increase from the first round" — these are internally-set valuations, not market-determined prices. No investor actually realized 40x — shares are illiquid.

**Fix:**
1. Add immediately after: "These valuations were set by the company for each round and are not based on independent third-party appraisals. No investor has realized any return — shares remain illiquid with no public market."
2. Replace "forty X increase" with neutral language: "The implied valuation has increased over successive rounds."

### MUST-FIX #3: Bonus Share Program Concerns

**Problem:** 50% bonus at $1M+ creates effective $4/share price vs. $6 stated price. Creates two-tier pricing that dilutes $10K investors. Language says "effective cost drops" which is price language.

**Fix:**
1. Verify bonus shares are fully described in PPM with identical terms
2. Add: "Bonus shares dilute all existing stockholders, including investors below bonus thresholds."
3. Reframe: Remove "effective cost per share drops" — say "additional shares at same offering price"

### MUST-FIX #4: Forward-Looking Statements Need Safe Harbor

**Problem:** Specific projections ($36.8M-$150.9M), TAM ($160B+), AUM targets — 506(c) does NOT benefit from PSLRA statutory safe harbor.

**Fix:**
1. Add formal forward-looking disclaimer at start of Scene 3
2. Individually qualify each projection
3. Slide 27 TAM: "Management's estimate, not independently verified"
4. Slide 29 Fincera: "Aspirational targets only. Not formed. No approvals. No AUM."

### MUST-FIX #5: Slide 52 Disclaimer Insufficient

**Add:**
1. Forward-looking statement risk language
2. "FY2025 data is unaudited and subject to change"
3. "No federal or state securities commission has approved these securities"
4. "Bonus shares subject to PPM terms"
5. "This presentation is not a substitute for the PPM"

## II. HIGH-PRIORITY ITEMS

### HIGH #1: "Show Your Receipts" Claims
Remove "No other player does that" — comparative claim in securities offering. Replace with: "We believe this level of transparency is uncommon in our industry."

### HIGH #2: Missing Conflict of Interest Disclosure
Carlos Cruz narrates AND holds 56% + sets his own compensation. Add: "I'm Carlos Cruz, CEO and majority shareholder. I have a direct financial interest in this offering."

### HIGH #3: Unaudited Financials Inadequate
First financial mention (Slide 16) must state: "These figures are from unaudited financial statements and are subject to adjustment."

### HIGH #4: "Same Round as Institutional" Claim
If institutional investors are not confirmed participants, REMOVE THIS CLAIM. If they are, qualify it.

### HIGH #5: Valuation Multiple Mixing
Slide 23 argues 11.5X multiple based on PROJECTED $100M revenue. Mixing actual and projected figures to make the multiple appear reasonable is misleading. Remove or clearly label both multiples.

## III. MODERATE ITEMS

- "What if you could own it?" — frames investment as aspirational/exciting. Add qualifier nearby.
- Missing accredited investor age/entity definitions — only mentions income and net worth.
- Missing state-specific notice requirements — some states require additional disclaimers.
- Transfer restriction language could be stronger.

✅ SEC investigation IS disclosed (just needs earlier placement)
✅ "No guarantee of returns" stated multiple times
✅ Illiquidity risk clearly explained
✅ Cash position and burn rate disclosed
✅ Controlling shareholder risk addressed
✅ Revenue figures sourced to specific documents
✅ Unaudited status mentioned (needs more prominence)
✅ PPM referral throughout
✅ "Accredited investors only" stated clearly
✅ Final disclaimer exists (needs expansion)

## SUMMARY: 5 MUST-FIX items before publishing. The script has a solid compliance foundation but the SEC investigation burial, 40X framing, and forward-looking statement gaps create real enforcement risk — especially with an active investigation.
