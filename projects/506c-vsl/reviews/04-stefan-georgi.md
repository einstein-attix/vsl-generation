# 🎯 STEFAN GEORGI (RMBC Method) — VSL CRITIQUE

## TA Fintech 506(c) Private Investment Script

---

## OVERALL RATING: 5.5/10

---

## 1. THE MECHANISM

**Current: No clearly articulated mechanism.** There are pieces — 488 AI events, subscription flywheel, deferred revenue — but no NAMED, FRAMED mechanism.

**Should be: "The AI Infrastructure Flywheel"** — AI generates data → data improves AI → better AI attracts subscribers → subscribers fund development → moat deepens daily. Name it. Build around it.

---

## 2. THE HOOK — Pattern-Interrupting Enough?

**4/10. Too polite. Too corporate.**

For warm traffic, the hook needs to break the FRAME of the relationship. They see themselves as *customers*. Make them see themselves as *insiders who are about to miss something*.

**Test this instead:**

> "Three hundred and eighty-one people invested in this company at a twenty-eight million dollar valuation. Today it's valued at one point one five billion. You were using the product. They were buying the company. This is the last time I'm going to explain the difference."

---

## 3. FASCINATIONS — Almost Nonexistent (2/10)

The script is: claim → data → claim → data. That's pitch deck cadence, not VSL cadence.

**Where fascinations SHOULD be:**
- **Slide 11:** "There's one number that separates TradeAlgo from every AI trading company on earth..."
- **Slide 21:** Lead with 40X as fascination, not chronological recitation. Move to SCENE 2.
- **Slide 28:** "There's a product that costs essentially nothing to produce — projected to drive 200K subscribers..."
- **Fincera:** "If one regulatory approval goes through, this unlocks an entirely new revenue model..."

---

## 4. PACING — Where It Drags

- **Scene 2 (1:30-4:00): A slog.** Product portfolio, subscriber counts, Trustpilot — they KNOW this.
- **Scene 3 (4:00-7:30): Interesting but flat.** "Earnings call" cadence. No emotional stakes.
- **Scene 6 (12:30-15:30): THREE FULL MINUTES of risk = momentum suicide.**

**Specific cuts needed:**
- Slides 8-9: Corporate identity. Compress to 15 seconds.
- Slide 19: GAAP revenue explanation. Move to PPM.
- Slide 22: Use of proceeds breakdown. One sentence max.
- Slide 45: Four additional risk cards. Compliance became copy.

---

## 5. OFFER STACK (5/10)

Bonus shares are genuinely compelling but presented as a table, not an escalating value revelation.

**Restructure:**
1. Lead with effective price, not face price
2. Frame bonus as "early insider pricing"
3. Add time/scarcity: "$120M hard cap. When it's raised, this price disappears."
4. Create tier identity — name the investment levels
5. Missing: What happens AFTER investment? Portal? Updates? Community?

---

## 6. COMPLIANCE/RISK — Without Killing Momentum

**A) Move risk acknowledgment EARLIER in micro-doses.** Don't save for one giant block.

**B) SEC investigation disclosed in Scene 2, not Scene 6.** "I need to disclose that the company is under SEC investigation. I'm telling you now rather than burying it because I respect your intelligence." — This BUILDS trust.

**C) Reframe risk section as "Due Diligence."** "Let's do what smart investors do — let's stress-test this." Same disclosures, different frame.

**D) Compress from 3 minutes to 90 seconds.** Detailed language belongs in PPM.

**E) Place CTA IMMEDIATELY after risks with bridge.** "You've seen the opportunity. You've seen the risks. If you're the kind of investor who can weigh both — here's how."

---

## 7. FIVE IMPROVEMENTS

### 1. OPEN WITH THE 40X STORY
Move Slide 21 to the hook. "381 people invested at $28.3M valuation. Today: $1.15B. You've been using the product they invested in."

### 2. NAME THE MECHANISM
Create the "AI Infrastructure Flywheel" — make it the central throughline. Every data point connects back to why the flywheel accelerates.

### 3. ADD IDENTITY-BASED FRAMING
Throughout: "You could keep being a customer who pays for the data... or you could be an owner who PROFITS from everyone who pays." Customer vs. Owner = emotional engine.

### 4. CUT 4 MINUTES, ADD 2 MINUTES OF STORY
Cut Scene 2 to 45 seconds. Cut GAAP explanation. Cut use-of-proceeds detail. ADD 90-second story about an early investor who was also a subscriber.

### 5. CREATE URGENCY
$120M hard cap. Closing date. Bonus tiers could be modified. "This presentation will be available for a limited time." Without urgency, warm leads become "I'll think about it" — the graveyard of private offerings.
