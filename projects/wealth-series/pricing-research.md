## High-Ticket Products ($10K–$100K+)

## Part 1: Research — How the Best VSLs Introduce Price

### 1. When Is Price First Mentioned?

| Framework | Price Mention Point | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Stansberry Research** ("End of America," "Death of the Dollar") | ~80–85% through | 45–90 min VSLs. Price doesn't appear until after the full crisis narrative, proof, credibility stack, and "what to do about it" solution. The first 75% is pure problem amplification + authority building. |
| **Agora Financial** (Palm Beach Letter, Oxford Club premium tiers) | ~75–85% | Agora's internal "magalog" structure adapted to video: Lead → Story → Mechanism → Proof → Offer → Price. Price is always in the final quintile. |
| **VectorVest / Motley Fool Premium** (Stock Advisor → Rule Breakers → Everlasting) | ~70–80% | Motley Fool's premium tiers ($10K–$50K for Everlasting/Blast Off) use webinar formats. Price appears after a long "stock picks that returned X%" proof section. |
| **Russell Brunson** (Perfect Webinar / High-Ticket) | Exactly at the "Stack Slide" — ~75% through | Brunson's framework is the most codified: Teaching → 3 Secrets → Transition → Stack → Price → Close. The stack slide appears ~45 min into a 60-min webinar. |
| **Alex Hormozi** ($100M Offers methodology) | ~80% | Hormozi advocates: "Make an offer so good people feel stupid saying no." Price is revealed only after the full value equation is established (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood / Time × Effort). |
| **Dan Kennedy** (Magnetic Marketing, GKIC high-ticket) | ~80–90% | Kennedy's rule: "Never discuss price until you've established value at least 10x the number you're about to say." Often the last 10–15% of the pitch. |

**Consensus: Price appears at 75–85% through the presentation.** Never before. The first ¾ is exclusively about building the problem, the solution, the mechanism, and the proof.

### 2. How Is Sticker Shock Mitigated for $25K–$100K Products?

The top frameworks use **5 layered techniques** (usually all of them, in sequence):

#### A. "Not For Everyone" Exclusivity Frame
> *"This isn't for everyone. In fact, we turn away more people than we accept."*

Stansberry's high-tier services (Stansberry Alpha, $5K–$50K/yr) position price as a **filter**, not a barrier. The psychology: if it's expensive, it must be good, and I must be worthy.

#### B. Massive Anchor Before the Reveal
Agora and Kennedy both teach: state a large number BEFORE your price.

- *"The average investor loses $47,000 per year to bad timing alone..."*
- *"A seat at a top hedge fund requires $1 million minimum and 2-and-20 fees..."*
- *"Our institutional clients pay $250,000 a year for this exact data..."*

The real price then feels small by comparison.

#### C. ROI / Payback Framing
Hormozi's value equation in action:
> *"If this system generates even ONE extra winning trade per quarter — at the position sizes our members use — that's $50,000 to $200,000 in additional returns. The entire cost pays for itself in a single trade."*

#### D. Per-Unit Time Breakdown
Brunson and Agora both break it down:
- Per year
- Per month  
- Per day
- Per trade / per signal

**Per-day is the most powerful** because it triggers the "cup of coffee" comparison instinct, but for high-ticket, **per-year** is the primary frame with per-day as a secondary kicker.

#### E. Pain of NOT Acting
Kennedy's "cost of inaction" close:
> *"What's it costing you right now to NOT have this? If you've lost even $10,000 in the last year from a bad trade, from being too slow, from not having the edge — you've already paid the price. You just didn't get anything for it."*

### 3. Breakdown Framing: Per Year / Month / Day?

**What works best for $25K–$100K products:**

| Tier | Primary Frame | Secondary Frame | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| $25,000 / 3yr | **$8,333/year** | $23/day | Annual feels like a "subscription." Daily triggers absurdity ("less than dinner"). |
| $50,000 / 3yr | **$16,667/year** | $46/day | Annual is the anchor. Daily comparison: "less than a nice lunch for two." |
| $100,000 / 3yr | **$33,333/year** | $91/day | Annual compared against institutional costs. Per-day compared against "what a single losing trade costs." |

**Key insight from Agora/Stansberry:** They NEVER lead with the total. They introduce the annual figure first, let it settle, THEN optionally reveal the total commitment. Some never even state the total lump sum — they frame it as an annual subscription with a 3-year lock.

**Hormozi's rule:** Break it down to the unit where it becomes obviously absurd NOT to buy. For trading signals: **per-signal** or **per-trade** is the killer frame.
> *"You'll receive approximately 200 signals per year. That's $42 per signal. Each signal is designed to produce $500 to $5,000+ in gains. You're paying $42 for the chance to make 100x that."*

### 4. How Do They Anchor Price Against Alternatives?

The best anchoring sequences (from real VSLs):

**Stansberry Pattern:**
1. State what institutional investors pay → "$250,000/year minimum for a Bloomberg Terminal + analyst team"
2. State what the information would cost to generate independently → "We employ 14 full-time analysts and spend $4.2 million per year on data feeds, research, and proprietary modeling"
3. State what a single mistake costs → "One bad earnings play can cost you $20,000 in an afternoon"
4. THEN reveal price

**Brunson "Stack Slide" Pattern:**
1. List each component with an individual value
2. Total the stack ("Total value: $197,000")
3. Say "But you're not going to pay $197,000"
4. Cross out numbers successively
5. Reveal actual price as a fraction

**Hormozi Pattern:**
1. Dream Outcome value → "What would an extra $500K/year in trading profits be worth to you?"
2. Perceived Likelihood boost → proof/testimonials
3. Time savings → "Instead of 40 hours/week analyzing charts, you get a signal in 30 seconds"
4. Effort reduction → "No analysis. No guessing. Just execute."
5. Price reveal as obvious bargain

**For TradeAlgo — DO NOT anchor against financial advisors** (per instruction). Instead, anchor against:
- The cost of building your own AI system (data scientists: $200K+/yr, computing: $50K+/yr, data feeds: $30K+/yr)
- The cost of bad trades / missed opportunities
- The value of the returns the AI has generated (backtest and live results)
- The cost of time spent doing manual analysis

### 5. The Optimal Sequence

Based on synthesis of all frameworks:

```
1. VALUE STACK (list everything included — make it overwhelming)
2. SOCIAL PROOF (results, testimonials, track record — right before price)
3. ANCHOR HIGH (what this would cost elsewhere / what it's really worth)
4. PRICE REVEAL (the annual figure, stated simply)
5. TIME BREAKDOWN (per day, per trade, per signal)  
6. COMMITMENT REVEAL (3-year term — AFTER the price feels small)
7. JUSTIFY & REFRAME (why 3 years makes it an even better deal)
8. COST OF INACTION (what NOT joining costs them)
9. SCARCITY/URGENCY (limited seats, closing enrollment)
10. CTA
```

**Critical insight: The 3-year commitment is revealed AFTER the price, not before.**

Why? Because if you say "3-year commitment" first, they mentally multiply. If you say the annual price first and let it feel reasonable, THEN say "and you lock in this rate for 3 full years," the commitment feels like a BENEFIT (rate protection) rather than a burden.

---

### 6. 3-Year Commitment: Before or After Price?

**After. Always after.**

Every high-performing framework reveals commitment length AFTER the price settles in the viewer's mind.

Stansberry's high-tier services: *"Your membership rate of $X,XXX per year is locked in for the next 36 months. While we reserve the right to raise rates for new members, your price will never increase during your commitment period."*

The commitment is reframed as:
- **Price protection** ("lock in today's rate")
- **Seriousness filter** ("we only want committed members")
- **Results framing** ("the system needs 3 years to deliver its full potential — just like compounding")

### 7. Language & Phrases the Best Closers Use

#### Price Minimization
- *"Your investment comes out to..."* (never "cost" or "price")
- *"For less than _____ a day..."*
- *"That's not even a rounding error on a single winning trade"*
- *"You probably spend more than that on _____ every month"*

#### Value Amplification
- *"If this produces even ONE additional winning trade..."*
- *"What's it worth to you to never miss another..."*
- *"Think about what you're really getting here..."*
- *"Let me put this in perspective..."*

#### Commitment Reframing
- *"You're not paying for a subscription — you're securing your seat"*
- *"This isn't an expense — it's an investment in your trading future"*
- *"Three years from now, you'll look back and realize this was the single best decision..."*
- *"Lock in" / "Secure" / "Guarantee your access"*

#### Exclusivity
- *"We only open enrollment [X] times per year"*
- *"We deliberately limit membership to protect signal quality"*
- *"Not everyone will qualify"*

#### Urgency (Kennedy / Agora style)
- *"The market doesn't wait for you to make up your mind"*
- *"Every day you delay is a day of signals you'll never get back"*
- *"This window closes [date]"*

We've invested over **twelve million dollars** and nearly a decade of development into what you're looking at right now.

But you don't have to pay twelve million dollars. You don't have to pay one million. You don't even have to pay a hundred thousand for the entry level.

Here's how it works.

We offer three tiers — **Atlas**, **Titan**, and **Everest** — each designed for a different level of trader.

**Atlas** gives you the core AI signal suite. Everything you need to start executing high-probability trades powered by our engine. Your investment for Atlas is **$8,333 per year.**

Let me say that again. For $8,333 a year, you get access to an AI system that took $12 million to build. That's **$23 a day.** Twenty-three dollars. You probably spend more than that on lunch. So let me ask you something.

What is it costing you right now to *not* have this?

Think about the last trade you got wrong. The one that kept you up at night. Think about the opportunities you missed because you didn't see them in time — or didn't trust your own analysis.

Those losses? Those missed gains? You already paid that price. You just got nothing in return.

Today, you can change that.

## Implementation Notes

- **Pacing:** Read at ~150 words/min for natural, authoritative delivery. This script runs ~3:30.
- **On-screen:** Show the annual price in large text. Show the per-day figure below it in smaller text. NEVER show the 3-year total on screen.
- **Visual stack:** Before price reveal, show a visual "value stack" slide listing all components with inflated individual values totaling $150K+, then cross through to the real price.
- **Tone:** Calm, confident, peer-to-peer. NOT hype. NOT screaming. The proof section did the heavy lifting — this section is the logical conclusion.
- **A/B test:** Test revealing Everest first (high anchor → Atlas feels cheap) vs. Atlas first (foot-in-door → upgrade path). Agora data suggests high-anchor-first converts better for ultra-high-ticket.
